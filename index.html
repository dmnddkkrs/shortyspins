Ledger Hardware Wallet — Review

⭐ Our Score: 4.6 / 5

🔐 Why You Need One

When your crypto sits on an exchange or a phone app, you don't truly control it — and if that platform gets hacked, your coins can vanish. A hardware wallet like Ledger keeps your private keys completely offline ("cold storage"), so hackers can't reach them. For anyone holding more than pocket change in crypto, it's the single most important security step.

🐘 Quick Verdict

Ledger is the most established name in hardware wallets — a French company operating since 2014, supporting 5,500+ coins and tokens. It's beginner-friendly through the Ledger app, yet secure enough for serious holders. Not the cheapest option, but the track record and ease of use make it our top pick for getting started safely.
